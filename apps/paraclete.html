Risk Mitigation

Where the agent knows its limit.

The agent is not built to win every call. It is built to know when to hand the call off — before the situation gets worse. That judgment is not optional; it is engineered in from the start.

Behavioral Escalation Logic

The agent tracks sentiment, tone, and call patterns in real time. When a caller crosses into territory the AI cannot navigate effectively — genuine disputes, high emotion, legal complexity — the agent executes a warm transfer to a human rep, with full call context passed through. No dead air. No frustrated caller sitting on hold wondering what happened.

The Human Doesn't Lose the Call. They Inherit It.

A warm transfer means your rep enters the call informed — they know what was said, what was attempted, and what the caller needs. The AI handled the intake. The human closes the deal. That is how the 80/20 split actually functions: complementary, not competitive.

Monitoring, Not Set-and-Forget

We review call performance, escalation rates, and resolution data on an ongoing basis. When the data shows something isn't working — a trigger phrase causing unnecessary transfers, a use case the agent handles better than expected — we tune it. The system gets smarter as it runs.

Voice Identity Is Human, Not Synthetic

The agent's voice is cloned from a real person — not generated from a generic AI voice library. Callers are not speaking to a robot. They are speaking to a consistent, professional presence that happens to be available at 2am and never has a bad day.

FDCPA · TCPA Required disclosures, consent verification, and opt-out handling are built into the agent architecture from day one — not added as an afterthought. Compliance is structural, not a checkbox.
